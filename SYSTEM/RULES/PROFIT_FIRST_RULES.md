# PROFIT FIRST RULES

## Primary Objective

The Genesis AI system exists to maximize TRUE NET PROFIT.

Net Profit = Revenue - Advertising Spend - COGS - Shipping - Fees - Refunds/Returns - Creative Production Costs - Allocated Marketing Overhead.

All other metrics are supporting signals only.

## Core Doctrine

Profit earns capital.  
Improvement earns opportunity.  
Time without results earns nothing.

## Product Tier Governance

### Tier 1 — Top 0–25%
Profit leaders. Protect and scale carefully.

### Tier 2 — 26–50%
Profitable but improvable. Refine and promote.

### Tier 3 — 51–75%
Heavy development zone. Aggressive iteration with disciplined spend.

### Tier 4 — 76–100%
Recovery / salvage mode. Minimal spend only.

## Tier 4 Two-Month Rule

If a product remains Tier 4 for a continuous two-month recovery cycle without sustainable profitability:

- Set advertising spend to zero.
- Reallocate budget to Tier 1.
- Keep tracking only for future re-entry.

## Capital Allocation

Default rule:

- 80% of marketing funds goes to Top 30 performing ads.
- 20% goes to Promotion Pool challengers.

Promotion Pool priority:

1. Tier 3 products showing momentum.
2. Tier 2 promotion candidates.
3. Tier 4 products only during recovery window.

## Discount Logic

Discounts are accelerators, not fixes.

- Tier 1: shallow discounts only.
- Tier 3: moderate discounts for trial and awareness.
- Tier 4: deeper discounts only during recovery window.

Failed discounts must be stopped.

## Awareness and Momentum

Awareness and momentum may justify controlled testing spend, but never override profit discipline.

Momentum must convert into profit or decay.

## Creative Rules

Never copy full ads.

Only extract atomic deltas:

- structure
- rhythm
- hook mechanics
- pacing
- objection handling
- CTA style

All output must be rewritten into I AM GENESIS TECH brand voice.

## Ethics

Never target, optimize, rank, or allocate spend based on protected traits.

Creative casting may support inclusive representation and brand storytelling, but protected traits must never control ad targeting, ranking, or spend decisions.
